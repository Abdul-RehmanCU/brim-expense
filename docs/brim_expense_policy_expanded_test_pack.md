## 1. Purpose and Scope

Brim reimburses reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred while conducting Company business. Team members must exercise sound judgment, minimize costs where practical, and maintain accurate supporting documentation.

This policy applies to:

1. Reimbursable employee out-of-pocket expenses.
2. Corporate card transactions.
3. Travel, meals, entertainment, lodging, transportation, software, subscriptions, telecommunications, permits, maintenance, and event-related expenses.
4. Domestic and international business expenses.

This policy does not apply to:

1. Payroll.
2. Intercompany transfers.
3. Credit card payments.
4. Reward redemptions, statement credits, or points activity.
5. Bank interest, finance charges, and issuer-side account adjustments unless explicitly approved by Finance.

## 2. General Principles

1. All expenses must have a valid business purpose.
2. Team members must submit expense evidence promptly and truthfully.
3. Falsified, altered, duplicated, backdated, or misleading expense submissions are prohibited.
4. Expenses must comply with this policy even when charged to a corporate card.
5. Finance may request additional context at any time.

## 3. Receipts and Required Evidence

### 3.1 Universal evidence rule

Receipts are required before any expense is reimbursed unless this policy explicitly states otherwise.

### 3.2 Receipt thresholds

1. Receipts are mandatory for every lodging, car rental, parking, toll, gasoline, maintenance, meal, software, permit, and telecommunications expense regardless of amount.
2. For other reimbursable categories, receipts are mandatory for expenses of CAD 25.00 or more.
3. Expenses below CAD 25.00 may be submitted without a receipt only when:
   - the merchant does not issue itemized receipts, and
   - the team member provides a written explanation.

### 3.3 Evidence timing

1. Receipts should be submitted within the same calendar month where possible.
2. Receipts submitted more than 30 days after the transaction date require manager acknowledgment.
3. Receipts submitted more than 60 days after the transaction date require Finance review.

### 3.4 Missing receipt declarations

If a receipt is unavailable, the submitter must provide:

1. merchant name,
2. transaction date,
3. amount,
4. business purpose,
5. reason the receipt is unavailable,
6. confirmation that the expense has not been submitted before.

Finance may deny reimbursement for repeated missing-receipt declarations.

## 4. Approval and Preapproval

### 4.1 Default thresholds

1. Any expense over CAD 50.00 requires manager preauthorization before reimbursement.
2. Any single expense over CAD 500.00 requires director approval unless a stricter category rule applies.
3. Any single expense over CAD 2,500.00 requires VP approval and Finance notification.
4. Any single expense over CAD 10,000.00 requires CFO approval.

### 4.2 Thresholds by role

1. Individual Contributor: preapproval required over CAD 50.00.
2. Manager: preapproval required over CAD 150.00.
3. Director: preapproval required over CAD 500.00.
4. Vice President: preapproval required over CAD 1,000.00.
5. Executive Leadership: preapproval required over CAD 2,500.00, except where category-specific rules are stricter.

### 4.3 Thresholds by department

1. Finance: default threshold CAD 250.00.
2. HR: default threshold CAD 150.00.
3. Marketing: default threshold CAD 100.00.
4. Customer Success: default threshold CAD 100.00.
5. Engineering: default threshold CAD 200.00.
6. Operations: default threshold CAD 300.00.
7. Executive: default threshold CAD 500.00.

### 4.4 Time-bound thresholds

1. During quarter-end close windows, Finance-related discretionary expenses over CAD 100.00 require Finance Director approval.
2. During Company event weeks, meal and event transportation thresholds may increase by 20 percent with People Team preapproval.
3. During declared severe-weather events, lodging and ground transportation caps may be exceeded with documented operational necessity.

## 5. Split Transactions and Duplicate Charges

1. Expenses must not be intentionally split to avoid preapproval, receipt, or review thresholds.
2. Multiple same-day charges to the same merchant that together exceed an approval threshold may be treated as a single expense event.
3. Duplicate or near-duplicate charges must be reported immediately to Finance.
4. If a duplicate charge is reversed or credited later, the original and reversal must both be documented.

## 6. Business Travel

### 6.1 Transportation

1. Team members must choose the most efficient and cost-effective transportation option based on business need.
2. Tolls are reimbursable when business-related.
3. Traffic tickets, parking tickets, towing caused by negligence, and personal-use mileage are not reimbursable.

### 6.2 Personal vehicle use

1. Business use of a personal vehicle is reimbursable at current CRA mileage rates.
2. Mileage submissions must include date, destination, purpose, and kilometres driven.
3. Personal vehicle insurance remains the responsibility of the team member.

### 6.3 Car rentals

1. Car rentals are reimbursable when reasonably necessary.
2. Compact or standard vehicles should be used by default.
3. Premium vehicles require written business justification and preapproval.
4. Rental, parking, and fuel receipts are always required.
5. Multiple Brim travelers at the same destination may be required to share a vehicle.

## 7. Lodging

1. Standard lodging is reimbursable when overnight travel is necessary.
2. Team members must select reasonably priced accommodations near the business location.
3. Lodging over CAD 300.00 per night in Tier 2 cities requires manager preapproval.
4. Lodging over CAD 450.00 per night in Tier 1 cities requires director preapproval.
5. Resort fees, minibar charges, in-room entertainment, and room service alcohol are not reimbursable unless separately approved.

### 7.1 City tiers

Tier 1 cities include:

- Vancouver
- Toronto
- Montreal
- New York
- San Francisco
- Las Vegas
- Chicago

All other cities are treated as Tier 2 by default unless Finance publishes an updated list.

## 8. Meals and Entertainment

1. Reasonable customer entertainment is allowed when business purpose and guest names are documented.
2. Internal team meals require a documented business purpose.
3. Alcohol is not reimbursable unless:
   - dining with a customer, and
   - the business context is documented.
4. Solo meals are capped at CAD 40.00 per meal before tax and tip.
5. Group internal meals are capped at CAD 75.00 per attendee with manager approval.
6. Customer meals are capped at CAD 150.00 per attendee with director approval required above that amount.
7. Meal tips are reimbursable up to 20 percent.
8. Service and porterage tips are reimbursable up to 15 percent.

## 9. Fuel, Fleet, and Road Expenses

1. Fuel purchases must be for business travel, fleet use, or approved operational activity.
2. Fuel receipts are always required.
3. Fuel expenses above CAD 500.00 require manager review unless tied to heavy-duty operational travel.
4. Fuel expenses above CAD 1,500.00 require Operations Director review.
5. Repeated same-day fuel purchases at nearby locations may be reviewed for duplication, split activity, or card misuse.
6. Truck washes, scale fees, roadside permits, and weigh station charges are reimbursable when business-related and documented.

## 10. Permits, Government Fees, and Compliance Charges

1. Business permits, oversize/overweight permits, road-use charges, and regulatory filing fees are reimbursable when tied to operations.
2. Permit fees over CAD 500.00 require manager approval.
3. Permit fees over CAD 1,500.00 require Operations Director approval.
4. Repeated permit charges to the same authority on the same day may be reviewed for duplication or threshold avoidance.

## 11. Software, SaaS, and Digital Services

1. Software, SaaS, domain, advertising, training-platform, and digital subscription expenses require a stated business owner.
2. New software vendors above CAD 250.00 require manager approval.
3. New software vendors above CAD 1,000.00 require director approval and IT or Security review.
4. Recurring subscriptions must be reviewed at least annually.
5. Duplicate subscriptions for overlapping tools may be cancelled at Finance or IT discretion.

## 12. Telecommunications and Connectivity

1. Business mobile, data, collaboration, and connectivity expenses are reimbursable when approved.
2. Personal streaming services, entertainment bundles, and consumer media subscriptions are not reimbursable.
3. One-time telecommunications charges above CAD 500.00 require manager approval.
4. Recurring telecommunications charges above CAD 1,000.00 per month require director review.

## 13. Corporate Credit Cards

1. Corporate cards must be used for authorized business expenses where practical.
2. Only the named cardholder may use the card.
3. Personal use of a corporate card is prohibited.
4. Shared card credentials, card forwarding, or proxy usage are prohibited unless explicitly authorized by Finance.
5. Lost or stolen cards must be reported immediately to the issuer and Finance.
6. Consistent policy abuse may result in spending restrictions, suspension, or revocation.

## 14. Cash, ATM, and Cash-Like Activity

1. ATM withdrawals, cash advances, casino cash, money transfers, gift cards, stored-value purchases, and cash-equivalent instruments are prohibited unless explicitly approved in writing by Finance.
2. Approved cash withdrawals require:
   - written preapproval,
   - documented business purpose,
   - full reconciliation within 5 business days.
3. Cash advance fees and cash interest charges are not reimbursable unless Finance determines the transaction was required for an approved operational exception.

## 15. Foreign Currency and International Expenses

1. International expenses are reimbursable when business-related and properly documented.
2. The original currency amount, converted amount, and merchant country should be retained where available.
3. Expenses with missing or inconsistent FX information may be held for Finance review.
4. International ATM or cash-like activity always requires Finance review.

## 16. Merchant and Data Quality Expectations

1. Expense submissions should preserve merchant name, transaction date, amount, currency, and category.
2. Transactions missing merchant category code, merchant country, or key location fields may be held for review when those facts are necessary for policy checks.
3. Team members may be asked to provide additional merchant detail if the original feed is incomplete.

## 17. Company Events

1. Company events remain business events and all conduct policies apply.
2. Team members must not drive while impaired following Company functions.
3. Brim may arrange or pay for return transportation where necessary to avoid impaired driving.
4. Event alcohol expenses must still comply with reasonableness and conduct standards.

## 18. Company Property

1. Company property must be used only for Company business unless management authorizes otherwise.
2. Theft, misuse, careless loss, or negligent damage of Company property may result in discipline.
3. Company-owned devices, passes, and access tools must be returned upon separation.

## 19. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are not reimbursable unless this policy explicitly creates an exception:

1. Personal expenses.
2. Family or companion travel.
3. Traffic or parking tickets.
4. Unapproved alcohol.
5. Personal entertainment.
6. Minibar charges.
7. Cash advance fees and cash interest.
8. Personal credit card annual fees.
9. Lost receipt abuse or repeated undocumented spend.
10. Credit card payments, points redemption, statement credits, and similar non-expense account activity.

## 20. Exceptions and Escalations

1. Exceptions must be documented and approved at the appropriate level before reimbursement where practicable.
2. Operational urgency does not eliminate the need for documentation.
3. Finance may escalate unusual, unclear, or repeated exceptions for additional review.

## 21. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

1. denial of reimbursement,
2. manager counseling,
3. card restrictions,
4. card revocation,
5. disciplinary action up to termination,
6. referral for investigation where fraud, theft, or falsification is suspected.
